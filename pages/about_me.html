Hello

My name is Michal and I am looking for a long-lasting work-relationship.

For me UX design is all about communication and honesty.

I am a User Experience Designer with 10 years of experience. I strongly believe in data, content driven design, testing with users, iteration and listening to people. In most cases, these methods work well enough for any project I am working on.

You will rarely hear me talk about "Saliency", "Double diamonds", "Lean Design Thinking" or any other lingo typical for people in my profession. Over the years of working in the industry I've learned that the UX vocabulary evolves faster than its methods.

How do I grow as a professional?

Since 2013 I am privileged to teach various UX topics to postgraduate students at SWPS University in Warsaw, Wroclaw, and Katowice. Helping students understand UX is a great enabler of my professional growth.

I read a lot of books, scour through various forums and subreddits related to the kind of work I am doing. Some of those are strictly about UX topics, though most are about psychology and cognitive science.

Last but not least, I learn on the job. Each project, test and analysis is an opportunity to learn something new for me UX design is all about communication and honesty.

Before I left to work in Gibraltar, I was a very active member of UX community. I participated in lectures, some of them were organised by me. I was mentoring a few people who wanted to become a part of the UX world. For a few years I was also running a UX Book Club. Have a look at my CV (link in the navigation) to read more about my after work activities. Since I am back in Warsaw, I am going to be slowly re-integrating myself with the community.

What would I like to do in the nearest future?

Work-wise, I would like to continue to focus on data and research driven design. I am invested in the digital world and my plan for the next couple of years is to take on more challenging products and services.

I would really like to get back to being a member of a UX community. Meet with other designers, listen to their stories and share my own experiences.
